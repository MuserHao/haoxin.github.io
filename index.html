< ABOUT.ME />

I'm a Senior Machine Learning Scientist at Apple, where I work on generative AI and multi-modal systems in the AI/ML organization.

I received my Ph.D. in Statistics from Purdue University in 2022, advised by Professor Michael Y. Zhu, where my dissertation focused on score-based generative models for image-to-image regression. Prior to that, I earned my M.S. in Statistics from the University of Wisconsin-Madison and my B.S. in Mathematics and Statistics from Nankai University.

My research interests span diffusion models, large language models, and computer vision. Currently, my work focuses on (i) generative modeling with diffusion and score-based methods, including training, alignment, and distillation, (ii) post-training and alignment of large language models and vision-language models through reinforcement learning and preference optimization, and (iii) building end-to-end perception and multi-modal AI systems that bridge research with production deployment.
